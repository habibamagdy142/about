About Us

A new chapter in Egypt's history begins !

The Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM) is one of the largest archaeological museums in the world, dedicated to showcasing Egypt's rich history and heritage. Located near the Pyramids of Giza, the museum houses over 100,000 artifacts, including the complete collection of King Tutankhamun’s treasures, many of which are being displayed for the first time. Designed to be a world-class cultural center, GEM offers visitors a unique journey through Egypt’s ancient civilization, from the prehistoric era to the Greco-Roman period. The museum's modern architecture, immersive exhibitions, and innovative technology create an unforgettable experience for history enthusiasts and tourists alike. The Grand Egyptian Museum aims not only to preserve Egypt’s invaluable treasures but also to inspire future generations with the wonders of its past.

From Concept to Reality

In January 2002, the Egyptian government announced an international competition to design a new museum near the Pyramids of Giza, now known as the Grand Egyptian Museum. The Irish firm Heneghan Peng Architects won the competition in 2003, and construction began in 2005. However, the project faced delays due to environmental, financial, and political challenges, especially after the 2011 revolution. Work resumed after political stability returned in 2014, supported by international funding. The museum is expected to fully open in mid-2024, becoming the largest archaeological museum in the world, showcasing over 100,000 artifacts, including the complete collection of King Tutankhamun’s treasures for the first time..

Our Vision And Objectives

To establish a distinguished museum that offers an enriching and comprehensive cultural experience. We are committed to preserving heritage, promoting knowledge, and fostering intellectual growth through innovative exhibitions, educational programs, and community initiatives. The new museum aspires to become a leading center for cultural exchange and scholarly excellence, while celebrating the creativity and ingenuity of the ancient Egyptians, and inspiring future generations through their timeless achievements.
